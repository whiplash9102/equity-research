## Investment Thesis — Three Reasons VMC Is 19% Overvalued

### 01 — Valuation at Peak with No Margin for Error
VMC trades at **18.8x EV/EBITDA**, above its own five-year historical average of 18.4x and at the top of its historical trading range. The DCF base case yields **$232** and the bull case **$252** — both sit below the current market price of $296. There is no scenario in the model that justifies buying at current levels.

### 02 — The IIJA Tailwind Is Already Priced In
Infrastructure stimulus from the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act has driven aggregates pricing up 10%+ in consecutive years. As IIJA disbursements peak in 2025–26 and housing starts remain depressed under 6.5% mortgage rates, the incremental demand argument weakens materially from here.

### 03 — Free Cash Flow Yield Below the Risk-Free Rate
VMC's FCF yield of **3.0%** sits 100 basis points below the 10-year US Treasury at 4.0%. Equity investors are accepting less yield than a risk-free government bond for a cyclical, asset-heavy materials company — a fundamental mispricing that a valuation correction will eventually close.
